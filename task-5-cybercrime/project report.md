What surprised me most while researching digital literacy and cybercrime was the sophisticated simplicity of social engineering in India, particularly through UPI "Collect Requests." As an Integrated M.Tech Computer Science student, I initially assumed cybercrime primarily involved complex hacking or code vulnerabilities. Realizing that a significant portion of financial theft relies on manipulating a user's psychological state—rather than breaking their encryption—was a profound wake-up call.

As a direct result, the habit I will personally change is my approach to credential management and mobile security. Despite my technical background in Python and C++, I realized I was becoming complacent with app permissions and public Wi-Fi. I will now strictly implement a "Zero-Trust" policy: I have committed to auditing my mobile app permissions weekly and will never use my Registration ID (25MIP10104) or university portals on unencrypted networks. This ensures my academic and professional assets at VIT Bhopal remain secure.
